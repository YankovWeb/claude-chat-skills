# Explain Ladder

One explanation at one level teaches little: too simple leaves gaps, too advanced bounces off. Climbing the same concept through five levels builds a real mental model — and naming the misconception at each level is what stops "understanding" that is actually a confident error. The misconception traps are the signature feature; never omit them.

## The five rungs

For the given concept, produce all five, clearly labeled:

### 🪜 Rung 1 — Curious kid (age ~10)
One concrete analogy from everyday physical life. No jargon at all. 3–5 sentences.
**Trap at this rung:** state the specific wrong belief the analogy plants (every analogy plants one), e.g. "This makes it sound like ___, which isn't true because ___."

### 🪜 Rung 2 — Smart adult, zero background
Introduce the 2–3 essential technical terms, each defined in the same breath it appears. The mechanism, correctly, in plain language.
**Trap:** the pop-science misunderstanding most adults carry about this topic.

### 🪜 Rung 3 — Student in the field
Now the real machinery: the core model, equation, algorithm, or process, with one worked micro-example. This rung should let someone pass an exam question.
**Trap:** the error students make on exams — usually a boundary condition or a confusion between two similar concepts.

### 🪜 Rung 4 — Practitioner
What matters in the real world: where the textbook model breaks, rules of thumb, trade-offs, what experienced people check first. Include one thing practitioners know that textbooks don't say.
**Trap:** the cargo-cult practice — something practitioners do "because everyone does" that's outdated or context-dependent.

### 🪜 Rung 5 — Expert / frontier
Open problems, current debates, the strongest competing schools of thought, and what would change the field if resolved. It's fine — good, even — to say "nobody knows" here.
**Trap:** the assumption experts themselves may be wrong about (state it as a live controversy, not a gotcha).

## Finishing moves
- **The thread**: one sentence tracing the single core idea that survived all five rungs unchanged — that's the true heart of the concept.
- **Self-test**: 3 questions of rising difficulty (rung 1/3/5) the user can answer to check which rung they actually reached.

## Craft rules
- Each rung must be *self-contained* — a reader at that level reads only their rung and gets a complete, non-lying picture.
- Rungs must not contradict each other; they refine, never retract. "What I said before was a lie" is a failed ladder — instead, rung 1 should be simplified in a direction rung 3 can extend.
- Keep rungs tight: the whole ladder should fit in one reading sitting.
