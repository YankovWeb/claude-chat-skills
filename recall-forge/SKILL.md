# Recall Forge

Re-reading feels like learning and does almost nothing. Three effects do nearly all the work of durable memory: **retrieval practice** (testing > reviewing), **spacing** (reviews just before forgetting), and **interleaving** (mixed practice > blocked). This skill forges raw material into those three shapes.

## Step 1 — Distill
Extract the 10–25 genuinely load-bearing items from the material (not everything — selection is a feature). Classify each: **fact** / **concept** / **procedure** / **discrimination** (X vs similar Y). The type determines the card format.

## Step 2 — Forge cards (the craft is in the card quality)

Card-writing laws:
- **Atomic** — one card, one idea. A card with "and" in the answer is usually two cards.
- **Effortful direction** — ask in the direction that's hard to recall, not hard to recognize. Often means making BOTH directions (term→meaning and meaning→term).
- **No orphans** — every card carries minimal context so it makes sense in isolation weeks later ("In X, why does…" not "Why does it…").
- **Cue ≠ answer leakage** — the question must not contain the answer's key word.

Formats by type:
- **Fact** → simple Q/A or cloze deletion: "The {…} effect means testing beats re-reading."
- **Concept** → "Explain why…" / "What would happen if…" cards (understanding probes, not definitions)
- **Procedure** → "What's the NEXT step after…" + one "spot the error in this sequence" card
- **Discrimination** → contrast cards: "X vs Y: which one …?" — these prevent the most common exam failure (confusing neighbors)

Deliver as a clean two-column table (front | back), plus — if the user uses Anki or similar — the same deck as importable text (front TAB back, one per line) in a file.

## Step 3 — Interleaved gauntlet
Build a 10-question self-test that MIXES topics and formats in shuffled order (never grouped by topic — the shuffle is the point) with 2–3 **transfer questions**: novel scenarios the material never mentioned but the concepts solve. Answers at the bottom, each with a one-line "if you missed this, revisit card #N".

## Step 4 — Spacing calendar
Concrete dates from today: **+1 day → +3 → +7 → +14 → +30 → +90**, with what to do at each (run gauntlet / shuffle cards / attempt the reversal). Rules to state:
- Review means *attempting recall before looking* — peeking first resets the clock's value.
- A miss at any stage sends that card back to the +1 track; hits keep climbing.
- Sessions should feel slightly unpleasant. Struggle during retrieval is the signature of encoding, not of failure ("desirable difficulty").

## Step 5 — The reversal (final boss)
The last calendar entry: user explains the whole topic from memory in their own words (out loud or written), THEN checks against the material. Offer to grade the explanation in a future chat.

If the material is in another language than the user's study language, ask which language the cards should be in before forging — mixed-language cards accidentally train translation instead of the content.
